The Timekeeper, responsible for recording the coming and passing of ages. She walks through eras, meets Arcanists, and leads them to escape the "Storm."
"I need you to... brave the 'Storm' with me."

Vertin (维尔汀) is the main protagonist of Reverse: 1999 and the character controlled by the player. She is currently known as the only person able to withstand "the Storm", having been given the title "Timekeeper" as a result. Throughout her time as the timekeeper, Vertin has rescued 63 unregistered Arcanists.

She wishes to find the truth behind the storm and the Foundation that she grew up in, this being a result of her recalling memories of her mother, whom she was previously unsure if she even existed at all, during her first storm. However when she went to ask the foundation , she was met with the response of "no such person".

Prologue

Vertin was taken in by the Foundation at one month old, and became a student at SPDM along with other young arcanists. She was incredibly shrewd and intellectual, leading to her dislike of class and would often skip, much to the frustration of the staff. She became close friends with fellow classmates Sonetto and Matilda. Despite being classified as an arcanist alongside the rest of the students, she herself was unable to use arcane skills.

As she got older, she became increasingly interested in the world beyond the Foundation, mainly the Storm, something that was heavily discouraged amongst the staff and students. One day, when she was 12 at the pre-parade assembly, the principal granted her one question, on account of being a "special student". She then proceeded to ask about the Storm, causing her to be sent to the guardhouse for the rest of the night,

After successfully fending off the Shamirs inhabiting the guardhouse, she realizes that she has now become hungry. Using the Shamirs to break the lock, she heads out to find food. On the way out, she meets another student held inside the guardhouse who calls himself The Ring. After bringing back food for the both of them, he asks Vertin how she ended up in the guardhouse, to which she tells him about the incident at the assembly. She explains that she had learned the word after hearing the janitor screaming it over and over while being escorted out of the school. She asked many people, but none bothered to answer. The Ring then shows her a stack of Manus Vindictae pamphlets he snuck out of the staff office that was written about the Storm. The two flip through the pages, finding a poem written at the back that greatly inspires them.

The next day in class, Vertin passes around a note inviting other students to who wanted to learn about the outside world to meet at George the Oak tree at 9. After class, Vertin gets confronted by a group of bullies, who threaten to report the note to the instructor. However, Vertin tells them that they have no proof that she wrote it, as the handwriting is different from hers. Angered, the bullies attack her, but she successfully fends them off and hides before the instructors come.

That night, The Ring and Vertin wait inside the oak tree. As they start to lose hope, they see a large group of students marching up to join them, including Mesmer Jr., Isabella, and Matilda. At first, the students are afraid of Vertin forming a rebellion, but she quickly dispels their worries. Vertin and the Ring then go on to explain to their classmates that the reason they gathered them all is because they all have their shared interest of knowing more about the world beyond the school, encouraging them to embrace the differences that the Student Handbook seeks to eliminate in them. The Ring and Vertin then share the poem they found the night before, and tells them while they may not be able to leave, they encourage them to remember who they were before going to SPDM so as to "rebuild" the world outside. Inspired, each of the students begin to share what fragments and memories they have of the outside world. Looking at their experiences that the Ring has written down, Isabella suddenly becomes inspired to sing it to the rhythm of their school song. The students then get the idea to sing the newfound melody at the parade ceremony as a way to get the Big Head to hear their collective wishes.

The day of the Parade, Sonetto confronts Vertin, taking note of how she wasn't in the dorms the night prior. Sonetto tells Vertin that she is aware of her scheming behind the instructor's backs, and encourages her to not do anything against the regulations. After the president's speech, the students are brought out to sing the school song. Against the odds, they all begin to sing the poem, shocking Sonetto. As the staff realize what they are doing, they desperately try to stop them from singing, to no avail. Security then force Isabella off the stage and spray the children with Dumbitter potion. Empowered by their desire to have their voices heard, more and more people join in on the singing against the potion, much to the fury of the principal. Vertin instructs them all to stand firm and hold their ground, while the principal instructs the guards to shoot tear gas above them. In the frenzy, Vertin is shot in the thigh with the tear gas, causing her to fall into a coma.

Prior to the main story, Vertin has been taking pictures of and recording the people she meets before they reverse in time. She accidentally discovers that the things inside her suitcase can withstand the storm with her, and slowly experiments with it, yet always failing whenever she tried bringing a person with her. Her last attempt was with Regulus who she thought was also an Arcanist due to her inaccurate reporting on the moon's phase through her ship's radio and just as she thought, Regulus is able to successfully bear the storm with her and ends up joining her as an ally.

Tender is the Night

Together with Regulus, and APPLe, Vertin travels back to 1929 through the storm, and after Sonetto joins back with her, their first mission is to interfere to an event where Arcanists get targeted and killed in Chicago, she heads to the scene and alongside Sonetto and APPLe, while Regulus who agrees to join the Foundation heads for the headquarters. However as she arrives to the scene, she finds that the past as it should have been was changed, and all the gang members who were supposed to target the Arcanists were killed by a mysterious girl. After following the clues left behind by the girl, and separating from Sonetto along the way, she arrives at the Storm Gathering in The Walden where they encounter the disguised Druvis III and find out the true identity of the Mysterious Girl as Schneider who she is chosen to duel with, as things get chaotic, Sonetto returns alongside Sotheby to support Vertin.

As Manus Vindictae block the entrances to The Walden, the group exits through the improvised exit where they run into the Manus members Arcana and Forget Me Not, and learns of Schneider's imprisoned sister Marian, together they rescue Marian, and while they are being pursued Vertin and Schneider remain in the labyrinth to fight off the attackers, while the rest escape and bring Marian to safety. However, the two are captured and brought back to Arcana by Druvis III.

Arcana does not forgive Schneider of her betrayal, and forces Vertin into joining the Manus, after which she orders her to kill Schneider, when she refused, Arcana offers her an orange, which takes Vertin under her spell, causing her to seriously injure Schneider without realising. Later, as the "storm" draws near and people begin experiencing the "storm" syndrome, Vertin is able to win the support of Druvis, and meets back with Schneider, whose body had been delivered to, and treated by Sotheby and Sonetto. The next day, Manus Vindictae gathers lots of civilians under the disguise of a relief camp and starts massacring them through explosives, with Regulus joining back with the team alongside X, there is a major confrontation between the two sides which ends in their victory.

Now that they are all gathered up after their victory, Vertin collects and records the wishes of everyone, and together they have a final banquet before the "Storm". Just as the "storm" begins, Schneider approaches Vertin one last time, and reveals her secret.

After arriving in 1913, the team is taken by the Foundation and Vertin is forcibly separated from her friends. Diagnosing her with Type ll Trauma from experiencing the same traumatic event repeatedly, she was taken to the Laplace Rehabilitation Center and forced into a medically induced coma to undergo Artificial Somnambulism Therapy to search her dreams and allegedly try to find the root cause of her trauma. Before going under, she gives Lilya a bottle of vodka and entrusts her friends safety with her.

An arcanist's work. Exhibited in the 1990s for 18 years. Completed in winter, on November 24. First exhibited in Utah, USA, and then moved to the Laplace Scientific Computing Center.

Medicine Pocket-- a talented biological researcher who likes to destroy things.
Are the research projects good for the society? All they care about is having fun.

They developed the Picrasma Candy, modified cellular structures, improved the cellular activity of arcanists, and spurred changes that affect the future of the arcanum world. However, injecting excessive amounts of reagents and conducting self-experimentation have also burdened them with numerous angry letters of complaint and reports.

When it comes to the feasibility of a project, Medicine Pocket has a rather comprehensive set of criteria. The project's scientific significance, its future prospects, and its positive value to the society... None of these matter. What matters is whether they are novel and profound enough to satisfy their willfulness.

Another talent of theirs is apparently even more well-known inside the office. The Computing Center specially equipped their lab with durable devices that are hard to bite, and training tools which they can use to release their excessive energy, such as soft rubber targets, roller tracks, and a Frisbee launcher.

✦ ✦ ✦ ✦ Star Reality

An arcanist's work. Exhibited in the mid-20th century for 15 years. Completed in Winter, December 26. Exhibited in Greater London, United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland.

Oliver Fog-- focused on cleaning fog during working hours, yet when the bell rings for the end of his shift, he will adamantly refuse all requests to work.

The memory of that winter day always jumps into his mind when he recalls the past. On that day, the fog outside was so thick that it seemed on the verge of breaking the window. It was so dark that no light could be seen. London looked as if it had been swallowed by a poisonous snake, and all that everyone could see was a foggy, steaming hell in its belly. Oliver's father kept coughing as if he was going to cough his lungs out of his body. His figure, which used to stand so tall, was now bent over and no different than a dry, withered branch. "It's my, as well as our responsibility..."

The hand resting on Oliver's shoulder disappeared, and his father put on his top hat again. The moment he pushed the door open, the greedy fog immediately devoured that once lofty figure.
...

Oliver picked up his pocket watch, wore his bowler hat, put on his coat and rested against his umbrella, just like that lofty figure he remembered. Now, there is a new Fogwalker in London, but there would never be another person like his father.

✦ ✦ ✦ ✦ ✦ Intellect Reality

An arcanist's work. Exhibited in the 1930s for 16 years. Completed in Spring, on May 30. Exhibited in the State of New York, USA, and later the exhibit was kept in the Institutum Lorentz.

X-- A genius who can create infinite possibilities with machinery. When he wants a cup of hot tea, it only takes him a push on a small iron ball.

His years at the orphanage taught X one truth: If you want something, you must take the initiative. Whenever he invents something new, there's always some unfortunate individual who ends up his test subject. He knew his appearance, along with just the right amount of cockiness, was something nobody could resist.

In order to protect what matters to you, it's hard to avoid doing something questionable. With convoluted and bewildering methods, along with a plethora of clues, under the intricate workings of the Goldberg machine, these chilling cases often go unresolved.

Enchanting items with precision is no small task, but it's simple enough for X. The unknown gift of his bloodline gives him the ability to "see" the points on each item that require arcane treatment. With some minor tweaking, machines can create endless possibilities in his hands.
